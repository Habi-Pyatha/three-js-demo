About the Universe

Space is unimaginably vast, containing billions of galaxies, each with billions of stars. Our own solar system is just a tiny part of the Milky Way galaxy.

Planets

Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune all orbit the Sun. Each planet has its own unique characteristics and atmosphere.

Stars

Stars are massive balls of plasma powered by nuclear fusion. Our Sun is a medium-sized star that provides the energy necessary for life on Earth.

Black Holes

Black holes have gravity so strong that not even light can escape them. They form when massive stars collapse under their own gravity.

Galaxies

Galaxies come in many shapes and sizes, including spiral, elliptical, and irregular. The Milky Way is estimated to contain over 100 billion stars.

Exploration

Humanity has explored the Moon, landed robotic missions on Mars, and sent spacecraft beyond our solar system. Future missions aim to return humans to the Moon and eventually reach Mars.
